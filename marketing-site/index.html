The real problem

Most families do not lack stories. They lack a way to finish them.

The memories are spread across siblings, parents, grandparents, phones, photo boxes, group texts, recipes, houses, and half-finished conversations. One person should not have to become the interviewer, archivist, photo organizer, audio editor, writer, and project manager.

StorySitting gives everyone a simple way to contribute, then turns the scattered pieces into a clear Story Map, a guided interview, and a finished keepsake.

Why now

The best time to capture the story is before the transition.

Before the house is sold Before the move Before the reunion ends Before the birthday passes Before the recipes lose their names Before “someday” becomes too late

Before the farm or home changes hands

Use the place itself as a prompt. Record the rooms, land, vehicles, tools, and memories while the family can still point to them.

Before the recipes lose their names

Turn recipes, kitchen objects, and ordinary family rituals into captions, voice excerpts, and keepsake pages.

Design direction

StorySitting is documentary, family archive, and heirloom product — not a generic SaaS page.

Image-led proofScenes of recording, photographing, objects, rooms, and land show the product before the copy explains it.
Physical detailsRecipes, old vehicles, bowls, photos, and recorders make the service concrete.
Warm authorityLarge editorial type, paper tones, and dark cinematic panels create trust without feeling like a photographer template.
