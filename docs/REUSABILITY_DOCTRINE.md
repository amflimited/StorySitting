# Capsule Reusability Doctrine

StorySitting has moved beyond proving that a Story Room can become a draft Capsule preview.

The next standard is reusability: every finished Capsule should make the next Capsule easier to sell, produce, review, and deliver.

## Success Levels

## Orbit

A Story Room can become a draft Capsule preview.

## Payload Success

The family receives a clear artifact that explains what was preserved, why it matters, and what they can keep or share.

## Customer-Side Landing

The family can see project progress without needing the founder to explain every step manually.

## Operator-Side Recovery

The founder or staff can finish the Capsule without reinventing prompts, sections, review rules, delivery language, or next steps.

## Reflight

The next Capsule should be faster because the previous Capsule produced reusable learning.

## Standing Rule

A Capsule is not fully complete until it improves the next Capsule.

Every project should produce:

- family progress visibility
- a delivery checklist
- reusable prompts
- reusable section patterns
- sample-safe proof candidates
- confusion points
- postflight learning
- a clearer next launch

## Human Approval Points

Keep human judgment around:

- final story tone
- sensitive family wording
- public sample use
- testimonial use
- final delivery quality

The system should prepare the next move, but the founder controls taste, permission, and final judgment.
